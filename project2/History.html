Korea’s history spans over 5,000 years, marked by the rise of powerful dynasties, cultural achievements, and periods of hardship. The Gojoseon Kingdom, founded in 2333 BCE, was the earliest recorded civilization, followed by the unification of the Three Kingdoms under Silla in 668 CE.

The Goryeo Dynasty (918-1392) introduced Buddhism and gave Korea its name. The Joseon Dynasty (1392-1897) was a golden age, bringing advancements in art, science, and the creation of Hangul, Korea’s unique alphabet. Despite Japanese occupation and the Korean War, South Korea has emerged as a vibrant, modern nation that still honors its rich historical heritage.
